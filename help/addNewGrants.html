Importing Old Grant Data and Adding New Grants

Federal grants are imported via downloads from the Federal RePORTER and the NIH ExPORTER. However, the following issues limit this process:

  • Internal grants and grants where the scholar is not the PI (e.g., K12) aren’t usually tracked by the federal databases.
  • Grants from foundations aren’t tracked either.
  • Grants from more than 10 years ago do not always appear in the federal databases.

An internal database can supplement these. To provide a live database connection, please see Adding New Data Sources.

Otherwise, you can enter Custom Grants manually via REDCap or via a spreadsheet program.

To enter grant information, one at a time, via REDCap:

  1. Go to the Wrangle menu and then click Add a Custom Grant.
  2. Select the record you intend to add grant information to.
  3. Enter as much information as you can about this grant. The Start Date and the Grant Type are especially important fields.
  4. These data will be re-summarized by the overnight script, so you’ll have to wait until the next morning to see these in your summaries.

    5. To enter grant information in bulk via a spreadsheet program:

    1. Go to the Wrangle menu and then click Add Custom Grants by Bulk.
    2. Download the template specified as a Comma-Separated Values (CSV) document. Open this document using your spreadsheet program.
    3. Enter in as much information as you can about this grant. The Start Date and the Grant Type are especially important fields.
    4. Save the file and upload the CSV when finished. Click the Upload button to submit the file.
    5. If there are any errors in your upload process, the website will tell you now. You may have to edit your spreadsheet a few times before getting the data into the right format.
    6. Once processed and accepted, these data will be re-summarized by the overnight script, so you’ll have to wait until the next morning to see these in your summaries.
